I am currently studying as a Schwarzman Scholar at Tsinghua University in Beijing. China fascinates me because of its rapid economic development, its vast AI/ML research output, and its widespread data availability. During my time here, I have focused on understanding the complex societal implications of automation. I will receive my M.A. in Global Affairs, Business, and Economics in July 2019. 

I received my B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Northeastern University in 2018. I completed four co-ops alongside my studies: software automation at SpaceX, firmware development at Tesla, printed circuit board design at Flextronics, and microprocessor design at AMD. I also conducted research with Prof. Chris Amato in the Lab for Learning and Planning in Robotics, and with Prof. David Kaeli in the Northeastern University Computer Architecture Research Group.
